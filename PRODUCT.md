# Volunteer Hub — Product Context

## Product

Volunteer Hub is a working multilingual platform for corporate and community
volunteering. It brings event discovery, registration, attendance, waitlists,
verified volunteer hours, certificates, and impact reporting into one workflow.

## Primary users

- Volunteers who need one reliable place to find opportunities and prove their impact.
- Coordinators who publish events, manage capacity, attendance, no-shows, and waitlists.
- HR and ESG teams who need trustworthy participation and impact metrics.
- Companies and NGO partners that need to recruit and coordinate volunteers.

## Core problem

Volunteer opportunities and participant data are usually scattered across chats,
mailing lists, forms, and spreadsheets. Volunteers miss events and wait too long
for certificates. Coordinators manually reconcile registrations, attendance,
hours, no-shows, and reports.

## Product promise

One transparent flow from publishing an opportunity to verified impact:
discover, register, attend, confirm hours, receive a verifiable certificate,
and report results.

## Personality

Practical, credible, human, and quietly confident. The interface should feel
operational rather than promotional.

## Design principles

1. Show the real workflow instead of making abstract claims.
2. Separate registrations from verified attendance and impact.
3. Give coordinators confidence about capacity, no-shows, and the waitlist.
4. Make the volunteer's next action and current status obvious.
5. Use progressive disclosure so advanced coordination tools do not clutter discovery.
6. Prefer specific operational language over generic SaaS copy.

## Anti-references

- Fake or inflated impact metrics.
- Generic dashboard decoration without a useful action.
- Spreadsheet-like friction and duplicated manual entry.
- Certificates issued before attendance is verified.
- Confusing administrator terminology or hidden status changes.

## Accessibility

- Aim for WCAG AA contrast.
- Preserve visible keyboard focus and semantic controls.
- Do not communicate status through color alone.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Keep core registration and coordinator workflows usable on mobile.
